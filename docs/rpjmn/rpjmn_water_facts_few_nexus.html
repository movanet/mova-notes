RPJMN Water Facts: Food-Energy-Water (FEW) Nexus Framework

Document Type: Source Material (Facts Only)
Source: RPJMN 2025-2029 - National Medium-Term Development Plan
Last Updated: 2025-10-30
Related Analysis: RPJMN_Water_Comprehensive_Analysis

Purpose

This document contains verbatim quotes and policy definitions regarding the Food-Energy-Water (FEW) Nexus framework from RPJMN 2025-2029. All content is presented in Indonesian original with English translation side-by-side. This is source material only - no analysis or commentary included.

1. Water as Foundation of National Self-Reliance

Source: Priority 2 - National Self-Sufficiency

Indonesian:

"Dalam kemandirian bangsa, Indonesia perlu fokus pada pencapaian swasembada dalam berbagai sektor krusial yang mencakup sektor pangan sebagai pilar kehidupan bangsa; sektor energi sebagai pendorong inovasi dan kemajuan; dan sektor air sebagai fondasi kehidupan yang berkelanjutan."

English:

"In national self-reliance, Indonesia needs to focus on achieving self-sufficiency in various crucial sectors including the food sector as the pillar of national life; the energy sector as a driver of innovation and progress; and the water sector as the foundation of sustainable life."

Strategic Positioning of Water:

  • Food: Pillar (pilar) of national life
  • Energy: Driver (pendorong) of innovation and progress
  • Water: Foundation (fondasi) of sustainable life

2. FEW Nexus Definition

Source: Priority 2 - National Self-Sufficiency

Indonesian:

"Swasembada air menjadi salah satu penerapan pendekatan Food, Energi, Water Nexus dalam rangka mewujudkan kemandirian bangsa terkait pemenuhan kebutuhan air berkelanjutan."

English:

"Water self-sufficiency is one application of the Food, Energy, Water Nexus approach to realize national self-reliance related to sustainable water needs fulfillment."

3. Source-to-Sea Integrated Management

Source: Priority 2 - Swasembada Air

Indonesian:

"Upaya mewujudkan swasembada air dilaksanakan secara terintegrasi dari hulu ke hilir dengan konsep source to sea melalui penyelamatan mata air kritis; konservasi dan pengelolaan air tanah; konservasi daerah tangkapan air melalui rehabilitasi hutan dan lahan; pembangunan tampungan air untuk pemanfaatan pertanian, peternakan, air minum, dan energi terbarukan; penyediaan pasokan air berkelanjutan."

English:

"Efforts to realize water self-sufficiency are implemented in an integrated manner from upstream to downstream with a source to sea concept through: rescue of critical springs; conservation and groundwater management; watershed conservation through forest and land rehabilitation; construction of water reservoirs for agricultural, livestock, drinking water, and renewable energy utilization; provision of sustainable water supply."

Key Components

  1. Upstream:

    • Critical spring rescue (penyelamatan mata air kritis)
    • Forest rehabilitation
    • Watershed conservation

  2. Midstream:

    • Reservoir construction (pembangunan tampungan air)
    • Water allocation across sectors:
      • Agriculture (pertanian)
      • Livestock (peternakan)
      • Drinking water (air minum)
      • Renewable energy (energi terbarukan)

  3. Downstream:

    • Sustainable supply provision
    • Wastewater treatment
    • Coastal protection

4. Smart Water Management and Water Accounting

Source: Priority 2 - Swasembada Air

Indonesian:

"Penerapan smart water management dan prinsip water accounting untuk menjaga neraca air pada tingkat wilayah sungai tidak dalam kondisi defisit/kritis/tertekan."

English:

"Implementation of smart water management and water accounting principles to maintain water balance at the river basin level not in deficit/critical/stressed conditions."

Water Balance Classification:

  • Defisit - Deficit
  • Kritis - Critical
  • Tertekan - Stressed

Objective: Prevent river basins from entering deficit/critical/stressed conditions

5. Integration Framework Recognized by RPJMN

Source: Priority 2 - Swasembada Air

Water-Food Linkages

  • Water storage for agriculture, livestock, irrigation
  • Agricultural water needs in river basin water accounting
  • Water scarcity as factor for food production

Water-Energy Linkages

  • Hydropower from water storage
  • Water for biofuel production and renewable energy
  • Energy for water pumping, treatment, distribution
  • Integration with 105 built dams for optimization

Water-Blue Economy Linkages

  • Coastal water quality management
  • Freshwater-seawater interface management
  • Aquaculture water needs
  • Marine ecosystem health (Seawater Quality Index)

6. Swasembada Water Zones

Source: Priority 4 - Regional Development

Indonesian:

"pengembangan kawasan swasembada pangan, air, energi, dan kawasan komoditas unggulan."

English:

"development of food, water, energy self-sufficiency zones, and priority commodity zones."

Regional Distribution of Swasembada Water Zones

  • Java: 11 swasembada water zones
  • Sumatra: Multiple zones (strategic role for food-water-energy)
  • Kalimantan: Multiple zones
  • Sulawesi: Multiple zones

Integration Approach:
Water self-sufficiency zones integrate food production, energy generation, and water security in spatial planning.
