Rawapening Actor-Policy Network Diagram

Interactive visualization of governance relationships, institutional networks, and policy contradictions in Rawapening Lake management.

Overview

This interactive network diagram visualizes:

  • 18+ actors across national, provincial, local, and community levels
  • 12+ policies and regulations at multiple governance tiers
  • 100+ relationships including coordination, conflicts, budget flows, information flows, and institutional gaps
  • 10 major paradoxes revealing governance failures and institutional pathologies

Features

Interactive Network Visualization

  • Force-directed graph with D3.js showing actor and policy relationships
  • Power-tier stratification: Nodes positioned vertically by power level (1-7)
  • Multiple relationship types: Coordination (solid), Conflict (dashed red), Budget (green), Information (blue), Gaps (dotted gray)
  • Zoom and pan: Navigate the network with mouse/trackpad
  • Drag nodes: Reposition actors and policies interactively

Filtering and Search

  • Relationship type filters: Show/hide specific relationship types
  • Node type filters: Filter by national, provincial, local, community actors, or policies
  • Theme filters (A-J): Focus on specific thematic areas:
    • A: Sedimentation & Lake Degradation
    • B: Multi-Stakeholder Coordination
    • C: Water Hyacinth (Enceng Gondok)
    • D: Agriculture, Farming, Livelihoods
    • E: Policy, Regulation, Legal Framework
    • F: Inter-Agency Gaps & Fragmentation
    • G: Community Perspectives & Land Rights
    • H: Budget, Financing, Resource Allocation
    • I: Monitoring, Data Systems, Information
    • J: Recommendations & Future Directions
  • Paradox highlighting: Visualize 10 major governance paradoxes
  • Search: Find actors or policies by name, code, or organization

Paradox Analysis

The diagram can highlight 10 major paradoxes:

  1. Authority Without Legitimacy: Kepmen 365 expansion without consultation
  2. Coordination Without Power: Pokja cannot override national regulations
  3. 800 ha Regulatory Black Hole: RTRW-Kepmen spatial contradiction unresolved since 2023
  4. BPDAS Institutional Orphaning: Authority loss with no replacement
  5. 18,000 vs 1,000 Dilemma: Zero-sum water allocation conflict
  6. Knowledge Without Action: Monitoring data produced but no enforcement
  7. Community as "Implementers Not Decision-Makers": Systematic exclusion from decision-making
  8. Land Data Black Hole: BPN excluded from Pokja, data fragmentation
  9. Regulatory Contradictions: Lake designation vs land certificates vs protected farmland
  10. Budget Allocation Injustice: 156B Rp to military, 0 Rp to displaced farmers

Bilingual Support

  • Toggle between English and Bahasa Indonesia
  • All actor names, organizations, quotes, and descriptions available in both languages
  • Preserves Indonesian technical terms (Pokja, enceng gondok, cekdam, etc.)

Detail Panel

Click any node to view:

  • Actor details: Name, organization, role, power level, themes
  • Key quotes: Direct evidence with Indonesian verbatim and English translations
  • Policy details: Full title, level, year, impact, implementing agencies
  • Relationships: All connections with relationship type and description

Usage Instructions

Opening the Visualization

  1. Open index.html in a modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari)
  2. The visualization will load automatically with all actors and policies visible

Navigation

  • Zoom: Scroll wheel or pinch gesture
  • Pan: Click and drag on empty space
  • Move nodes: Click and drag individual nodes
  • Reset view: Click "Reset View" button in header

Interacting with Nodes

  • Single click: Select node and view details in right panel
  • Double click: Highlight node and all its connections (dims others)
  • Hover: See tooltip with basic information

Filtering

  1. By Relationship Type: Check/uncheck boxes in left sidebar
  2. By Node Type: Check/uncheck national, provincial, local, community, or policies
  3. By Theme: Click theme buttons (A-J) to filter by thematic area (multi-select)
  4. By Paradox: Select from dropdown to focus on specific governance paradox

Search

  1. Type in search box in left sidebar
  2. Results appear below with matching actors/policies
  3. Click any result to focus and zoom to that node

Language Toggle

  • Click language toggle button in header to switch between English and Indonesian
  • All text, labels, and details update immediately

Data Structure

The network data (data/network-data.json) contains:

  • Nodes: Actors (government officials, community members, organizations) and Policies (regulations, programs)
  • Edges: Relationships with type, weight, description, and optional paradox link
  • Paradoxes: 10 governance paradoxes with descriptions and related actors/policies
  • Themes: 10 analytical themes (A-J) with descriptions

Node Properties

  • id: Unique identifier (e.g., "PAN", "KEPMEN365")
  • type: "actor" or "policy"
  • name/nameID: English and Indonesian names
  • tier (actors): national, provincial, local, community
  • powerLevel (actors): 1-7 scale
  • level (policies): presidential, ministerial, provincial, district, sectoral, budget
  • themes: Array of theme codes (A-J)
  • keyQuotes: Array of quote objects with bilingual text

Edge Properties

  • source: Source node ID
  • target: Target node ID
  • type: coordination, conflict, budget, information, gap
  • weight: Relationship strength (1-5)
  • description/descriptionID: Bilingual description
  • bidirectional: Boolean (true for two-way relationships)
  • paradox: Optional link to paradox ID

Key Insights from Visualization

Power Hierarchy

The vertical positioning reveals a clear 7-tier power structure:

  1. Tier 7 (Top): National ministries (KLHK, PUPR), Military (Kodam)
  2. Tier 6: National agencies (BWS)
  3. Tier 5: Provincial government (Pokja, Setda, Provincial Dinas)
  4. Tier 4: Local government (Bupati, Secretariat), BPDAS (reduced authority)
  5. Tier 3: Community organizations (Forum Petani), Downstream irrigation
  6. Tier 2: Individual community members (farmers, fishers, elders)

Coordination Fragmentation

  • Pokja established (Pergub 610/2019) but BPN excluded → data black hole
  • BPDAS authority loss post-2017 with no replacement
  • Vertical coordination breakdown: Provincial requests to national level unaddressed since 2023

Regulatory Contradictions

  • Kepmen 365 (2020) expands lake boundary by 67% vs RTRW Provincial (800 ha gap)
  • Lake designation (Kepmen 365) vs Land certification (PTSL 4,292 parcels)
  • Conservation mandate (Kepmen 365) vs Protected farmland (LSD)

Budget Injustice

  • 156 billion Rp → Kodam/TNI (water hyacinth contract)
  • 0 Rp → Displaced farmers (2.5-year farming ban, continued taxation)

Community Exclusion

  • 68-72 years historical knowledge (SWE, GIT, MBP)
  • Systematically excluded from Pokja decision-making
  • "Policy makers are not us. We are just implementers in the field." (GIT-Q005)
