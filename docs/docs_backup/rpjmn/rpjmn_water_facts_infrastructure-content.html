3. Three Core Water Infrastructure Programs (KPU)

Source: Priority 2 - Swasembada Air

KPU #14: Konservasi Sumber Daya Air (Water Resource Conservation)

Indonesian:

  • Watershed management and protection
  • Groundwater conservation
  • Critical spring rescue operations
  • Forest and land rehabilitation

KPU #15: Pengembangan SPAM Terintegrasi Hulu ke Hilir

Indonesian:

"Pengembangan SPAM Terintegrasi Hulu ke Hilir"

English:

"Integrated Drinking Water Supply System Development from Upstream to Downstream"

SPAM Definition:

  • Indonesian: Sistem Penyediaan Air Minum
  • English: Drinking Water Supply System

KPU #16: Penyediaan dan Pengawasan Sanitasi Aman, Berkelanjutan, dan Berketahanan Iklim Berbasis CWIS

Indonesian:

"Penyediaan dan Pengawasan Sanitasi Aman, Berkelanjutan, dan Berketahanan Iklim Berbasis CWIS"

English:

"Provision and Supervision of Safe, Sustainable, and Climate-Resilient Sanitation Based on City-Wide Inclusive Sanitation"

CWIS Characteristics:

  • Safe (aman)
  • Sustainable (berkelanjutan)
  • Climate-resilient (berketahanan iklim)
  • Covers on-site and off-site sanitation
  • Includes fecal sludge management (IPLT) and wastewater treatment (IPAL)

5. Nature-Based Solutions Integration

Source: Priority 3 - Infrastructure Development

Indonesian:

"Pembangunan infrastruktur akan mengoptimalkan infrastruktur terpadu dengan memperhatikan pendekatan solusi berbasis alam untuk mengurangi risiko bencana dan perubahan iklim, sekaligus menjaga kesinambungan pembangunan dalam jangka panjang."

English:

6. Financing Mechanisms

Source: Priority 3 - Infrastructure Development

Green and Blue Finance

Indonesian:

"didorong diversifikasi produk dan layanan yang sesuai dengan kebutuhan masyarakat dan perkembangan zaman, seperti pembiayaan perumahan, pembiayaan berkelanjutan (hijau dan biru)"

English:

"diversification of products and services according to community needs and contemporary developments is encouraged, such as housing financing, sustainable financing (green and blue)"

Financing Structure (National Infrastructure 2025-2029)

Total Requirement: Rp 6,445 trillion

  • Government: 37% (National and local budget - APBN, APBD)
  • State-Owned Enterprises: 21% (BUMN infrastructure investment)
  • Private Sector: 42% (Investment and PPPs - largest share)
  • International Partners: Climate finance, development banks

7. Lake Management Programs

Source: KPU #22 (02.17.01) - Priority 2

Lake Water Quality Management Program

Indonesian:

"Peningkatan pengelolaan kualitas air di sungai dan danau"

English:

"Improvement of water quality management in rivers and lakes"

Target Outcomes:

  1. Increased water quality data availability for rivers and lakes
  2. Improved surface water quality through systematic monitoring
  3. Enhanced environmental parameter testing capacity
  4. Strengthened water pollution control in industrial sectors

Implementing Agencies:

  • Ministry of Environment and Forestry / Environmental Management Agency
  • Ministry of Public Works (water resource management)
  • Ministry of Agriculture (agricultural runoff control)
  • Ministry of Maritime Affairs and Fisheries (aquatic ecosystems)
  • Ministry of Health (drinking water quality)
  • Ministry of Higher Education, Science and Technology (research and monitoring)
  • National Research and Innovation Agency
  • National Food Agency
  • Local Governments

Funding Sources:

  • State Budget (Belanja K/L)
  • Regional Budget (APBD)

9. City-Wide Inclusive Sanitation (CWIS) Adoption

Source: Priority 2 - Swasembada Air

Indonesian:

"penyediaan dan pengawasan sanitasi aman, berkelanjutan, dan berketahanan iklim berbasis Citi-Wide Inclusive Sanitation"

English:

"provision and supervision of safe, sustainable, and climate-resilient sanitation based on City-Wide Inclusive Sanitation"

CWIS Principles:

  1. Citywide coverage - Not just networked areas, entire urban area
  2. Inclusive approach - Prioritizing low-income and informal settlements
  3. Service-oriented - Focus on sanitation outcomes, not just infrastructure
  4. Integrated solutions - Combining on-site (septic) and off-site (sewerage)
  5. Climate resilience - Adaptation to flooding, drought, sea-level rise

10. Source-to-Sea Integrated Management

Source: Priority 2 - Swasembada Air

Indonesian:

"Upaya mewujudkan swasembada air dilaksanakan secara terintegrasi dari hulu ke hilir dengan konsep source to sea melalui penyelamatan mata air kritis; konservasi dan pengelolaan air tanah; konservasi daerah tangkapan air melalui rehabilitasi hutan dan lahan; pembangunan tampungan air untuk pemanfaatan pertanian, peternakan, air minum, dan energi terbarukan; penyediaan pasokan air berkelanjutan."

English:

"Efforts to realize water self-sufficiency are implemented in an integrated manner from upstream to downstream with a source to sea concept through: rescue of critical springs; conservation and groundwater management; watershed conservation through forest and land rehabilitation; construction of water reservoirs for agricultural, livestock, drinking water, and renewable energy utilization; provision of sustainable water supply."

Key Components:

  1. Upstream: Critical spring rescue, forest rehabilitation, watershed conservation
  2. Midstream: Reservoir construction, water allocation across sectors
  3. Downstream: Sustainable supply provision, wastewater treatment, coastal protection

11. Smart Water Management and Water Accounting

Source: Priority 2 - Swasembada Air

Indonesian:

"Penerapan smart water management dan prinsip water accounting untuk menjaga neraca air pada tingkat wilayah sungai tidak dalam kondisi defisit/kritis/tertekan."

English:

"Implementation of smart water management and water accounting principles to maintain water balance at the river basin level not in deficit/critical/stressed conditions."

Technology Integration:

  • Digital monitoring and control systems
  • River basin-level water balance tracking
  • Real-time deficit prevention
  • Integration with 105 built dams for optimization

12. Implementing Agencies

Source: Priority 2 and 3

Primary Agency:

  • Kementerian Pekerjaan Umum dan Perumahan Rakyat (Ministry of Public Works and Public Housing)
    • All dam projects
    • SPAM systems
    • Infrastructure construction

Supporting Agencies:

  • Kementerian Lingkungan Hidup dan Kehutanan - Water quality, watershed management
  • Badan Pengelola Dana Lingkungan Hidup - Climate finance for water projects
  • Provincial/Local Governments - SPAM operations, sanitation service delivery
  • Badan Regulator Air Minum dan Sanitasi - Regulatory oversight (new)

13. Land Value Capture (LVC) Financing

Source: Priority 2 - Swasembada Air

Indonesian:

"Langkah-langkah ini diwujudkan melalui penerapan pendekatan pengelolaan kawasan (Land Value Capture), Program Percepatan Penyediaan Air Minum, dan Program Percepatan Pembangunan Sanitasi Permukiman."

English:

"These steps are realized through implementation of area management approaches (Land Value Capture), Drinking Water Provision Acceleration Program, and Settlement Sanitation Development Acceleration Program."
