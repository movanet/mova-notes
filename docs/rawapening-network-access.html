Overview

This interactive network diagram visualizes the complex governance dynamics in Rawapening Lake (Rawa Pening) management, Central Java, Indonesia. Based on CSIRO research analyzing stakeholder interviews and policy documents.

What's Included

  • 18+ actors across national, provincial, local, and community levels
  • 12+ policies and regulations spanning multiple governance tiers
  • 100+ relationships including coordination, conflicts, budget flows, information flows, and institutional gaps
  • 10 major paradoxes revealing governance failures and institutional pathologies
  • 199 direct quotes from 6 stakeholder interviews (Indonesian + English translations)
  • 10 analytical themes (A-J) covering sedimentation, coordination, water hyacinth, agriculture, policy, etc.

üéØ Key Insights

Power Hierarchy (7 Tiers)

The vertical positioning reveals:

  1. Tier 7: National ministries (KLHK, PUPR), Military (Kodam)
  2. Tier 6: National agencies (BWS)
  3. Tier 5: Provincial government (Pokja, Setda, Provincial Dinas)
  4. Tier 4: Local government (Bupati), BPDAS (reduced authority)
  5. Tier 3: Community organizations (Forum Petani)
  6. Tier 2: Individual community members (farmers, fishers, elders)

Coordination Fragmentation

  • Pokja established (Pergub 610/2019) but BPN excluded ‚Üí data black hole
  • BPDAS authority loss post-2017 with no replacement
  • Vertical coordination breakdown: Provincial requests unaddressed since 2023

Regulatory Contradictions

  • Kepmen 365 (2020) expands lake 67% vs RTRW Provincial (800 ha gap)
  • Lake designation vs Land certification (PTSL 4,292 parcels)
  • Conservation mandate vs Protected farmland (LSD)

Budget Injustice

  • 156 billion Rp ‚Üí Kodam/TNI (water hyacinth contract)
  • 0 Rp ‚Üí Displaced farmers (2.5-year farming ban)

Central Finding

"Rawa Pening's environmental crisis stems fundamentally from governance failure rather than technical deficit, with 8 major paradoxes revealing institutional pathologies including coordination fragmentation, regulatory polycentrism, strategic ambiguity, and systematic environmental justice violations."
