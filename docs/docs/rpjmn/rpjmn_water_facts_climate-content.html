Purpose

This document contains verbatim quotes and statistics regarding climate-resilient water management from RPJMN 2025-2029. All content is presented in Indonesian original with English translation side-by-side. This is source material only - no analysis or commentary included.

1. Water as Climate Priority Sector

Source: Priority 8 - Environmental Harmony and Climate Adaptation

Indonesian:

"Pembangunan berketahanan iklim diimplementasikan untuk menyeimbangkan aspek ekonomi, sosial, dan lingkungan. Implementasi aksi pembangunan berketahanan iklim diterjemahkan sebagai tindakan yang mengurangi potensi kerugian ekonomi akibat ancaman bahaya, kerentanan, dampak, dan risiko perubahan iklim yang difokuskan pada empat sektor prioritas, yaitu sektor kelautan dan pesisir (ocean climate), air, pertanian, serta kesehatan."

English:

"Climate-resilient development is implemented to balance economic, social, and environmental aspects. Implementation of climate-resilient development actions is translated as actions that reduce potential economic losses due to hazard threats, vulnerability, impacts, and climate change risks focused on four priority sectors: ocean and coastal sector (ocean climate), water, agriculture, and health."

2. Coastal Flooding and Sea Level Rise

Source: Chapter I - Baseline Evaluation

23 Million People at Risk by 2050

Indonesian:

"Data dari Climate Central menyebutkan bahwa sekitar 23 juta penduduk pesisir di Indonesia diproyeksikan akan menghadapi banjir laut tahunan pada tahun 2050 akibat kenaikan muka air laut."

English:

"Data from Climate Central states that approximately 23 million coastal residents in Indonesia are projected to face annual sea flooding by 2050 due to sea level rise."

3. Altered Precipitation Patterns

Source: Chapter I - Baseline Evaluation

Indonesian:

"Di sisi lain, jumlah hari hujan cenderung menurun, sementara intensitas hujan lebih lebat dan ekstrem. Perubahan ini meningkatkan risiko banjir di daerah hilir saat kejadian hujan ekstrem serta memperpanjang periode kekeringan."

English:

"On the other hand, the number of rainy days tends to decrease, while rainfall intensity is heavier and more extreme. These changes increase flood risk in downstream areas during extreme rainfall events and prolong drought periods."
