Executive Summary

Indonesia's National Medium-Term Development Plan (RPJMN) 2025-2029 positions water self-sufficiency (swasembada air) as a foundational pillar of national development, integrating water security across eight national priorities. This analysis examines the comprehensive approach to water governance, infrastructure development, and climate adaptation, including institutional reforms such as the establishment of an independent Water and Sanitation Regulatory Body.

Baseline Conditions

Water Resources and Access Statistics (see RPJMN_Water_Facts_Rural and RPJMN_Water_Facts_Baseline):

  • 4.77% of villages have safe sanitation access
  • 18.9% of villages have safe drinking water access
  • 70% of household water sources are contaminated (2022 data)
  • 56.1% of population (Java) has access to only 6.3% of water resources
  • 23 million coastal residents projected to face annual flooding by 2050
  • 12.7 million hectares of critical land no longer function as water regulation media

Development Targets (2025-2029) (see RPJMN_Water_Facts_Infrastructure):

  • Urban piped water access: 24% → 51.36% (114% increase)
  • Safe sanitation access: 10.21% → 30% (194% increase)
  • Water Quality Index: 54.59 → 72.77 (33% improvement)
  • National Water Security Index: 3.48 → 3.75

Institutional Reform (see RPJMN_Water_Facts_Regulatory):

  • Establishment of independent Water and Sanitation Regulatory Body (Badan Regulator Air Minum dan Sanitasi) with operational and financial independence
  • Full value chain coverage: raw water → drinking water → wastewater treatment
  • Four main functions: benchmarking, tariff recommendations, quality monitoring, transparency enhancement

1. Strategic Framework: Water Self-Sufficiency and FEW Nexus

1.1 Water as Foundation of National Self-Reliance

Source: Priority 2 - National Self-Sufficiency (Swasembada Pangan, Energi, Air)

Verbatim (Indonesian):

"Dalam kemandirian bangsa, Indonesia perlu fokus pada pencapaian swasembada dalam berbagai sektor krusial yang mencakup sektor pangan sebagai pilar kehidupan bangsa; sektor energi sebagai pendorong inovasi dan kemajuan; dan sektor air sebagai fondasi kehidupan yang berkelanjutan."

Translation:

"In national self-reliance, Indonesia needs to focus on achieving self-sufficiency in various crucial sectors including the food sector as the pillar of national life; the energy sector as a driver of innovation and progress; and the water sector as the foundation of sustainable life."

Strategic Positioning: Water is distinguished as the foundational sector (fondasi) for sustainable development, differentiating it from food (pillar) and energy (driver). This elevates water to the highest strategic significance.

1.2 Food-Energy-Water (FEW) Nexus Approach

Verbatim (Indonesian):

"Swasembada air menjadi salah satu penerapan pendekatan Food, Energi, Water Nexus dalam rangka mewujudkan kemandirian bangsa terkait pemenuhan kebutuhan air berkelanjutan."

Translation:

"Water self-sufficiency is one application of the Food, Energy, Water Nexus approach to realize national self-reliance related to sustainable water needs fulfillment."

Integration Framework: Indonesia explicitly adopts FEW Nexus as operational framework, recognizing:

  • Water enables agricultural production (food security)
  • Water generates renewable energy (hydropower)
  • Agriculture requires water and energy inputs
  • Energy powers water pumping and treatment
  • Climate change affects all three sectors simultaneously

1.3 Source-to-Sea Integrated Management

Verbatim (Indonesian):

"Upaya mewujudkan swasembada air dilaksanakan secara terintegrasi dari hulu ke hilir dengan konsep source to sea melalui penyelamatan mata air kritis; konservasi dan pengelolaan air tanah; konservasi daerah tangkapan air melalui rehabilitasi hutan dan lahan; pembangunan tampungan air untuk pemanfaatan pertanian, peternakan, air minum, dan energi terbarukan; penyediaan pasokan air berkelanjutan."

Translation:

"Efforts to realize water self-sufficiency are implemented in an integrated manner from upstream to downstream with a source to sea concept through: rescue of critical springs; conservation and groundwater management; watershed conservation through forest and land rehabilitation; construction of water reservoirs for agricultural, livestock, drinking water, and renewable energy utilization; provision of sustainable water supply."

Key Components:

  1. Upstream: Critical spring rescue, forest rehabilitation, watershed conservation
  2. Midstream: Reservoir construction, water allocation across sectors
  3. Downstream: Sustainable supply provision, wastewater treatment, coastal protection

2. Institutional Reform: Water and Sanitation Regulatory Body

Note: For complete verbatim quotes and source material, see RPJMN_Water_Facts_Regulatory.

2.1 Establishment of Independent Regulatory Authority

Source (Indonesian):

"Pembentukan Badan Regulator Air Minum dan Sanitasi memperkuat fungsi pengaturan, pengawasan, dan advisori yang selama ini terfragmentasi dan belum berjalan optimal, sekaligus menjadi langkah debottlenecking untuk menciptakan tata kelola lebih efektif dan efisien. Sejalan dengan visi swasembada air, badan ini dirancang memiliki independensi operasional serta finansial untuk memastikan ketersediaan air yang berkeadilan dan berkelanjutan bagi seluruh rakyat Indonesia, mencakup pengelolaan terpadu dari hulu ke hilir mulai dari penyediaan air baku, penyediaan dan distribusi air minum, hingga pengolahan air limbah domestik."

Translation:

"The establishment of the Water and Sanitation Regulatory Body strengthens regulatory, supervisory, and advisory functions that have been fragmented and not running optimally, while becoming a debottlenecking step to create more effective and efficient governance. In line with the vision of water self-sufficiency, this body is designed to have operational and financial independence to ensure equitable and sustainable water availability for all Indonesian people, covering integrated management from upstream to downstream starting from raw water supply, drinking water supply and distribution, to domestic wastewater treatment."

2.2 Four Main Regulatory Functions

Verbatim (Indonesian):

"Badan ini memiliki peran utama yaitu:

  1. Penilaian kinerja layanan melalui sistem benchmarking
  2. Pemberian rekomendasi tarif dan retribusi berbasis prinsip keadilan, efisiensi ekonomi, model pemulihan biaya, dan keterjangkauan
  3. Pemantauan terhadap standar kuantitas dan kualitas layanan
  4. Peningkatan transparansi melalui pelaporan publik dan pengelolaan data"

Translation - Four Main Roles:

  1. Service performance assessment through benchmarking system
  2. Tariff and retribution recommendations based on principles of equity, economic efficiency, cost recovery model, and affordability
  3. Monitoring of service quantity and quality standards
  4. Transparency enhancement through public reporting and data management

Related Note: Tantangan Regulasi Pelayanan Air di Indonesia - Current regulatory challenges

2.3 Institutional Characteristics

Key Features:

  • Independent - Operational and financial independence from government line agencies
  • Comprehensive scope - Full value chain: raw water → drinking water → wastewater
  • Source-to-sea integration - Aligns with integrated water management approach
  • Debottlenecking role - Addresses current fragmented governance structure
  • Equity-focused - Mandate to ensure "berkeadilan" (equitable/just) water availability

Comparison with Current Fragmentation:

  • Before: Regulatory functions scattered across Ministry of Public Works, Ministry of Health, Ministry of Environment, local governments
  • After: Unified regulatory authority with clear mandate and independence

2.4 Incentive and Disincentive Mechanisms

Verbatim (Indonesian):

"Untuk mendukung peningkatan layanan, badan ini menjalankan mekanisme insentif dan disinsentif yang diharapkan dapat memacu kinerja operator, efisiensi biaya, dan optimalisasi sumber daya."

Translation:

"To support service improvement, this body will run incentive and disincentive mechanisms that are expected to spur operator performance, cost efficiency, and resource optimization."

Performance-Based Regulation:

  • Tied to service quality and quantity standards
  • Targets operator efficiency and resource optimization
  • Balances consumer protection with provider viability

Related Note: Perbedaan Fitur Regulasi Sumber Daya Air dengan Pelayanan Air - Distinguishing water resources from water services regulation

2.5 Restructuring of Wastewater Governance

Verbatim (Indonesian):

"Penataan Kewenangan Penyelenggaraan Urusan Air Limbah Domestik... bertujuan untuk menciptakan pembagian peran dan tanggung jawab yang lebih jelas antara pemerintah pusat, provinsi, dan kabupaten/kota dalam penyelenggaraan layanan air limbah domestik."

Translation:

"Restructuring of Domestic Wastewater Service Authority... aims to create clearer division of roles and responsibilities between central government, provinces, and districts/cities in providing domestic wastewater services."

Multi-Level Coordination:

  1. Central government - Policy and standards setting
  2. Provincial government - Oversight and support to struggling districts
  3. District/city government - Operational service delivery (UPTD, BLUD, BUMD, PDAM)

Provincial Role Strengthening:

"Provinsi akan menjalankan fungsi pengawasan untuk memastikan kinerja layanan di tingkat kabupaten/kota sesuai dengan standar yang telah ditetapkan. Provinsi juga diberikan kewenangan lebih untuk ikut serta dalam menyediakan layanan air limbah domestik pada kabupaten/kotanya yang kesulitan."

Translation:

"Provinces will carry out supervisory functions to ensure service performance at district/city level meets established standards. Provinces are also given greater authority to participate in providing domestic wastewater services to districts/cities that have difficulty."

3.2 Three Core Water Infrastructure Programs (KPU)

Kegiatan Prioritas Utama (Priority Activities):

KPU #14: Konservasi Sumber Daya Air (Water Resource Conservation)

  • Watershed management and protection
  • Groundwater conservation
  • Critical spring rescue operations
  • Forest and land rehabilitation

KPU #15: Pengembangan SPAM Terintegrasi Hulu ke Hilir

  • SPAM = Sistem Penyediaan Air Minum (Drinking Water Supply System)
  • Integrated approach from source to tap
  • Full upstream-downstream coverage
  • Primary drinking water infrastructure program

KPU #16: Penyediaan dan Pengawasan Sanitasi Aman, Berkelanjutan, dan Berketahanan Iklim Berbasis CWIS

  • CWIS = City-Wide Inclusive Sanitation (international best practice)
  • Safe (aman), sustainable (berkelanjutan), climate-resilient (berketahanan iklim)
  • Covers on-site and off-site sanitation
  • Fecal sludge management (IPLT) and wastewater treatment (IPAL)

3.5 Financing Mechanisms

Green and Blue Finance:

Verbatim (Indonesian):

"didorong diversifikasi produk dan layanan yang sesuai dengan kebutuhan masyarakat dan perkembangan zaman, seperti pembiayaan perumahan, pembiayaan berkelanjutan (hijau dan biru)"

Translation:

"diversification of products and services according to community needs and contemporary developments is encouraged, such as housing financing, sustainable financing (green and blue)"

  1. Green bonds - For wastewater treatment, water-efficient technologies, watershed restoration

Lake management is positioned within the blue economy framework, linking freshwater lake ecosystems to broader aquatic resource strategies.

3.6.4 Lake Ecosystem Conservation and Carrying Capacity

Environmental Policy Context:

Lakes are managed under integrated environmental quality improvement framework:

Verbatim (Indonesian):

"Peningkatan kualitas lingkungan hidup dan tata ruang dilakukan untuk memastikan ketersediaan daya dukung dan daya tampung lingkungan yang sejalan dengan pertumbuhan ekonomi"

Translation:

"Improvement of environmental quality and spatial planning is conducted to ensure availability of environmental carrying capacity and assimilative capacity in line with economic growth"

Carrying Capacity Management:

  • Daya dukung lingkungan (environmental carrying capacity) - Maximum development level lake ecosystems can sustain
  • Daya tampung lingkungan (environmental assimilative capacity) - Maximum pollution load lakes can process without degradation
  • Spatial planning integration to balance tourism/economic development with ecological protection

Related Note: Carrying capacity and pollution assimilative capacity framework provides technical methodology.

3.6.5 Lake Management Challenges and Opportunities

Key Challenges:

  1. Eutrophication from agricultural runoff and urban wastewater discharge
  2. Sedimentation reducing water storage capacity and navigability
  3. Invasive species disrupting native lake ecosystems
  4. Tourism pressure on fragile lake environments
  5. Climate change altering lake hydrology and thermal stratification
  6. Multi-jurisdictional coordination (lakes often span multiple administrative boundaries)

Integrated Management Opportunities:

  1. Payment for Ecosystem Services (PES) schemes where downstream beneficiaries fund upstream watershed conservation
  2. Community-based lake management involving local fishing communities and indigenous groups
  3. Real-time monitoring systems using IoT sensors for water quality
  4. Nature-based solutions including constructed wetlands for nutrient removal
  5. Integrated lake basin planning coordinating water allocation, tourism, conservation, and food production

Connection to National Water Security: Lake management contributes to National Water Security Index targets (3.48 → 3.75) by:

  • Serving as strategic water reserves during dry seasons
  • Supporting agricultural irrigation systems
  • Providing drinking water sources for surrounding communities
  • Maintaining environmental flows for downstream ecosystems

"Pembangunan berketahanan iklim diimplementasikan untuk menyeimbangkan aspek ekonomi, sosial, dan lingkungan. Implementasi aksi pembangunan berketahanan iklim diterjemahkan sebagai tindakan yang mengurangi potensi kerugian ekonomi akibat ancaman bahaya, kerentanan, dampak, dan risiko perubahan iklim yang difokuskan pada empat sektor prioritas, yaitu sektor kelautan dan pesisir (ocean climate), air, pertanian, serta kesehatan."

Implementation of climate-resilient development actions is translated as actions that reduce potential economic losses due to hazard threats, vulnerability, impacts, and climate change risks focused on four priority sectors: ocean and coastal sector (ocean climate), water, agriculture, and health."

Water as 1 of 4 Climate Priority Sectors - Recognizes water's critical vulnerability to climate change.

4.3 Coastal Flooding and Sea Level Rise Threats

23 Million People at Risk by 2050:

Verbatim (Indonesian):

"Data dari Climate Central menyebutkan bahwa sekitar 23 juta penduduk pesisir di Indonesia diproyeksikan akan menghadapi banjir laut tahunan pada tahun 2050 akibat kenaikan muka air laut."

Translation:

"Data from Climate Central states that approximately 23 million coastal residents in Indonesia are projected to face annual sea flooding by 2050 due to sea level rise."

Combined Threat:

  • Sea level rise: +50 cm projected
  • Land subsidence: 1-15 cm/year in Jakarta & Semarang
  • Total relative rise: Up to 3.5 meters in worst-affected areas

Vulnerable Administrative Units:

  • 311 kabupaten/kota (regencies/cities)
  • 1,643 kecamatan (sub-districts)
  • 6,367 desa/kelurahan (villages)
  • 30% of coastal villages already showing displacement signs (2013-2023)

Water Infrastructure Implications:

  • Saltwater intrusion into freshwater sources
  • Coastal SPAM infrastructure flooding
  • Groundwater contamination
  • Need for giant sea wall and mangrove restoration

10.4 Conclusion

RPJMN 2025-2029 presents Indonesia's comprehensive water sector strategy, positioning water as a national self-sufficiency priority alongside food and energy (see RPJMN_Water_Facts_FEW_Nexus). The plan integrates eight key dimensions:

  1. Strategic positioning - Water as "fondasi kehidupan berkelanjutan" (foundation of sustainable life)
  2. Institutional reform - Independent regulatory body with full value chain coverage (see RPJMN_Water_Facts_Regulatory)
  3. FEW Nexus integration - Holistic food-energy-water planning approach
  4. Climate resilience - Water as 1 of 4 climate priority sectors (see RPJMN_Water_Facts_Climate)
  5. Infrastructure development - 114% increase in piped water, 194% in sanitation access (see RPJMN_Water_Facts_Infrastructure)
  6. Quality improvement - 33% increase in Water Quality Index target
  7. Rural development - "Rumah Murah Bersanitasi Baik" program (see RPJMN_Water_Facts_Rural)
  8. Source-to-sea integration - Upstream watershed to coastal protection approach

Key Implementation Considerations:

Institutional Development: Establishment of Badan Regulator Air Minum dan Sanitasi (Water and Sanitation Regulatory Body) with operational and financial independence addresses fragmented authority and provides unified oversight for the water-sanitation value chain.

Rural Water Access: With 4.77% of villages having safe sanitation and 18.9% having safe water access, scaling up requires substantial resource mobilization through Dana Desa and multi-level government coordination.

Financing Requirements: Achieving infrastructure targets requires mobilizing 42% private sector financing (Rp 2,707 trillion) through green/blue bonds and innovative mechanisms like Land Value Capture.

Climate Adaptation Needs: With 23 million coastal residents projected to face annual flooding by 2050, altered precipitation patterns, and 12.7 million hectares of degraded watersheds, climate-resilient infrastructure design is essential.

Implementation Pathway: Success depends on translating policy objectives into funded annual work plans, building institutional capacity, maintaining implementation continuity, and ensuring equitable access across urban and rural populations.
