RPJMN Water Facts: Baseline Conditions and Challenges

Last Updated: 2025-10-30

Purpose

This document contains verbatim quotes, data, and statistics regarding Indonesia's water resources baseline conditions and challenges from RPJMN 2025-2029. All content is presented in Indonesian original with English translation side-by-side. This is source material only - no analysis or commentary included.

1. Water Availability and Regional Disparities

Source: Chapter I - Development Evaluation and Challenges

Total Water Resources

  • Surface water: 2,783.3 billion m³/year
  • Groundwater potential: 20,903.3 billion m³/year
  • Total potential: Over 23,686 billion m³/year

Regional Distribution

Indonesian:

"Wilayah padat penduduk seperti Pulau Jawa, yang menampung 56,1 persen populasi nasional, hanya memiliki 6,3 persen dari total ketersediaan air permukaan. Sebaliknya, wilayah-wilayah dengan sumber daya air melimpah seperti Papua (27,6 persen), Kalimantan (28,5 persen), dan Sumatera (26,1 persen) menampung populasi yang jauh lebih kecil."

English:

"Densely populated areas such as Java Island, which houses 56.1 percent of the national population, only has 6.3 percent of total surface water availability. Conversely, regions with abundant water resources such as Papua (27.6 percent), Kalimantan (28.5 percent), and Sumatera (26.1 percent) house far smaller populations."

Water-Population Imbalance Table

Region % Population % Water Availability Imbalance Ratio
Java 56.1% 6.3% 1:0.11 (8.9x deficit)
Papua ~2% 27.6% 1:13.8 (massive surplus)
Kalimantan ~6% 28.5% 1:4.75 (large surplus)
Sumatera ~21% 26.1% 1:1.24 (slight surplus)

2. Watershed Degradation

Source: Chapter I - Development Evaluation and Challenges

12.7 Million Hectares of Critical Land

Indonesian:

"keberadaan hutan yang berfungsi sebagai pengatur tata air alami tertekan akibat tingginya pertumbuhan penduduk dan aktivitas ekonomi yang tidak berkelanjutan. Saat ini, 12,7 juta hektare lahan kritis sudah tidak berfungsi sebagai media pengatur tata air."

English:

"the existence of forests that function as natural water regulators is under pressure due to high population growth and unsustainable economic activities. Currently, 12.7 million hectares of critical land no longer function as water regulation media."

Forest Cover Loss Projection

Indonesian:

"tutupan hutan pada habitat spesies kunci di sebelah barat garis Wallacea akan menyusut dari 80,3 persen pada tahun 2000 menjadi 49,7 persen pada tahun 2045, terutama pada wilayah Sumatera dan Kalimantan."

English:

"forest cover in key species habitat west of the Wallace Line will shrink from 80.3 percent in 2000 to 49.7 percent in 2045, especially in Sumatra and Kalimantan regions."

Forest Cover Loss: 30.6 percentage point decline projected (2000-2045)

3. Water Contamination Emergency

Source: Chapter I - Development Evaluation and Challenges

70% of Household Water Sources Contaminated

Indonesian:

"Sekitar 70 persen dari 20 ribu sumber air minum rumah tangga yang diuji tercemar (Badan Pusat Statistik, 2022)."

English:

"Approximately 70 percent of 20 thousand household drinking water sources tested were contaminated (Statistics Indonesia, 2022)."

Contamination Statistics:

  • 70% contamination rate
  • 20,000 sources tested
  • Testing period: 2020-2022
  • Source: Badan Pusat Statistik (Statistics Indonesia), 2022

Regional Context:

  • Indonesia is highest-polluted country in Southeast Asia (WHO 2021)

4. Climate-Induced Hydrological Stress

Source: Chapter I - Development Evaluation and Challenges

Hydrometeorological Disaster Risk

Disaster Risk Classification:

  • Zero provinces classified as low disaster risk
  • 168 kabupaten/kota in high disaster risk category
  • 346 kabupaten/kota in medium disaster risk category

5. Historical Infrastructure Achievements (2020-2024)

Source: Chapter I - Development Evaluation and Challenges

Infrastructure Progress:

  • Irrigation system development supporting agricultural productivity
  • Clean water and sanitation access expansion contributing to poverty reduction
  • Drainage infrastructure development in rural areas
  • Program Kali Bersih (Clean River Program) improving Environmental Quality Index

Conservation Achievements:

  • 51.14 million hectares terrestrial protected areas (26.85% of land)
  • 29.9 million hectares marine conservation areas (9.2% of territorial waters)
  • 665,049.5 hectares increase in marine conservation during RPJMN 2020-2024

6. Built Dams Infrastructure

Source: Priority 2 - Swasembada Air

Existing Infrastructure:

  • 105 built dams currently operational
  • Used for optimization through smart water management integration
